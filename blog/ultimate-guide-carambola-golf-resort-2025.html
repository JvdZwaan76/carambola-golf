A Legacy of Excellence: The History and Design of Carambola Golf Course St. Croix

Nestled on a former sugar plantation in the northwest hills of St. Croix, Carambola Golf Course opened in 1966 as part of a visionary development by Laurence Rockefeller. The course's architect, Robert Trent Jones Sr., called it one of his finest creations‚Äîand for good reason. Spanning 7,181 yards from the back tees, it weaves through undulating terrain, tropical foliage, and sparkling ponds, with the Caribbean Sea as your constant backdrop.

A golfer tees off at sunrise on Carambola's championship course, where every hole offers stunning Caribbean views and strategic challenges designed by Robert Trent Jones Sr.

What sets Carambola apart? Its "pleasantly testing" layout combines strategic bunkers, water hazards on multiple holes, and elevation changes that demand precision and creativity. Recent upgrades, including new equipment and improved greens that roll smoothly and challenge even low-handicappers, have golfers buzzing.

Rated 4 stars by GOLF Magazine and praised on platforms like TripAdvisor for its "relaxing and enjoyable" vibe, the course often feels like your private oasis‚Äîdeserted fairways mean no rush, just pure immersion in nature. Birdwatchers, bring binoculars: Pelicans, herons, and ducks frequent the ponds, adding to the serene, sustainable atmosphere we prioritize at Carambola.

Hole-by-Hole Highlights: What to Expect on the Course

Carambola Golf Course St. Croix isn't just about the views; it's a test of strategy and stamina. Here's a sneak peek at some standout holes:

One of Carambola's signature oceanfront holes showcasing the dramatic Caribbean Sea views that make this Robert Trent Jones Sr. design truly special.

Hole 1 (Par 4, 410 yards): The Welcome

A gentle opener downhill to a wide fairway, but beware the left-side bunker‚Äîset the tone with a solid drive.

Hole 7 (Par 3, 175 yards): The Signature

One of the signature holes, requiring a carry over water to an elevated green framed by palm trees. Recent reviews call it "challenging yet fair," with the sea breeze adding an extra layer of excitement.

Hole 12 (Par 5, 550 yards): The Risk-Reward

A risk-reward par 5 with uphill shots and cross-water hazards‚Äîgo for the green in two if you're feeling bold, but the lush valley views might distract you!

Finish strong with a dogleg right, aiming for the clubhouse ruins in the distance. It's a fitting end to a round that's both exhilarating and relaxing.
